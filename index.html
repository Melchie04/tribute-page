Manny Pacquiao

One of the Greatest Professional Boxers of All Time

Here's a timeline of Manny Pacquiao’s career life:

  • 1978 - Born Emmanuel Dapidran Pacquiao in Kibawe, Bukidnon, Philippines

  • 1992 - Moved to Manila to became a part of the Philippine national amateur boxing team with an amateur record of 60-4

  • 1995 - Made his professional debut in a four-round bout in the light flyweight division against Edmund "Enting" Ignacio and won

  • 1998 - His first major boxing honor came when he won a bout against Chatchai Sasakul of Thailand and earned the ‘World Boxing Council’ (WBC) flyweight title

  • 2001 - He faced Lehlohonolo Ledwaba for the ‘International Boxing Federation’ (IBF) junior featherweight title and won by a technical knockout. This was his second major boxing title, which he defended four times.

  • 2003 - In a career-defining fight for the Lineal and The Ring Featherweight championship in Texas, he defeated Marco Antonio through a technical knockout. He defended this title twice.

  • 2004 - He faced Juan Manuel, the holder of WBA and IBF featherweight titles, in Las Vegas. This bout was scored a draw.

  • 2005 - He fought the Mexican legend Erik Morales for the WBC international and IBA super featherweight titles in the super featherweight division. He lost the 12-round match.

  • 2006 - He defeated Morales in a rematch in Las Vegas. He defended his WBC international title against Oscar Larios and won.

  • 2008 - He fought for the WBC lightweight title and won the title by defeating David Diaz in the ninth round via knockout. He also defeated Oscar De La Hoya, the six-division world champion, in the welterweight division.

  • 2009 - He defeated Ricky Hatton and won The Ring’s junior welterweight title. Then in a fight termed ‘Firepower,’ he defeated Miguel Cotto via technical knockout in the twelfth round at the ‘MGM Grand,’ Las Vegas, and won the WBO welterweight title.

  • 2010 - Pacquiao’s successful foray into politics, with him being proclaimed congressman of the Sarangani district.

  • 2012 - He faced Timothy Bradley to defend his WBO welterweight title in a 12-round fight. Bradley won the fight.

  • 2013 - He ran unopposed for his second term as a congressman.

  • 2014 - He faced Timothy Bradley, at the ‘Grand Garden Arena’ in Nevada. This time, Pacquiao won and re-established himself as a strong fighter.

  • 2016 - The Commission of Elections announced him as one of the winners of the Philippine Senate seat.

  • 2018 - He signed with Al Haymon's ‘Premier Boxing Champions’ (PBC) promotion.

  • 2019 - He won the WBA (Super) welterweight title.
